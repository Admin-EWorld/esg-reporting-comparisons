    The 5 Best Workiva Alternatives for Mid-Market Private Equity ESG Reporting

    Workiva is the undeniable leader in integrated financial, GRC, and ESG reporting for large, publicly traded enterprises. However, for a mid-market Private Equity (PE) firm focused specifically on managing portfolio-level ESG data—often on a tighter budget and with a need for private-market specialization—Workiva can be overkill.

    The ideal alternative offers:

    1. Private Market Focus: Tools tailored for portfolio company data collection and investor reporting (e.g., LP reporting, due diligence).
    2. Ease of Deployment: Faster setup and lower implementation costs than enterprise platforms.
    3. Auditability: Robust data trails to prepare portfolio companies for future assurance and compliance.

    Vendor Deep Dive: Pros & Cons

    1. Novata: The Private Market Specialist

    Novata was created specifically to standardize and simplify ESG data management for private capital markets (PE, VC, and private credit). It acts as a neutral intermediary, helping GPs streamline data collection from often unsophisticated portfolio companies (PortCos).

    Key Features:

    • Streamlined data collection and automated workflows for PortCos.
    • Built-in benchmarks to compare portfolio company performance against peers.
    • ESG due diligence solution for deal teams.
    • Built-in carbon accounting tools.

    Pros:

    • Highest Relevance to PE: Platform and support are designed for the unique GP/PortCo dynamic.
    • Advisory Support: Offers built-in guidance to help firms select and track the right metrics.
    • Simplified Metrics: Helps firms and PortCos start with core, measurable metrics rather than overwhelming frameworks.

    Cons:

    • Lacks advanced features like an API for deep integrations or a mobile app.
    • Pricing is opaque and tailored, requiring a direct quote.

    Verdict: For mid-market PE firms whose main pain point is collecting and benchmarking data across their portfolio for LP reporting, Novata is the most specialized alternative to Workiva.

    2. Pulsora: For Assurance-Ready Compliance

    Pulsora targets mid-market firms and enterprises facing new, stringent regulations like CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) and the SEC Climate Rule. Its core strength is data auditability, ensuring every metric can be traced back to its source for third-party assurance.

    Key Features:

    • Cell-level audit trail for ultimate data defensibility.
    • Pre-built templates for major compliance frameworks (CSRD, TCFD, GRI).
    • Automated Scope 1, 2, and 3 carbon accounting.
    • Supplier engagement portal for collecting Scope 3 data.

    Pros:

    • Unrivaled Auditability: Excellent choice for firms preparing for regulatory assurance requirements.
    • High-Touch Support: Praised for “white-glove service” during complex implementation.
    • Strong focus on integrating supply chain (Scope 3) emissions.

    Cons:

    • Higher cost and significant implementation time compared to simpler tools.
    • Some users report limitations in advanced data visualization and analytics.

    Verdict: Choose Pulsora if your portfolio companies are global, face demanding EU compliance (CSRD), or if your firm requires the absolute highest level of data defensibility for future audits.

    3. Sustain.Life: The Decarbonization Driver

    Sustain.Life focuses heavily on carbon accounting and providing actionable climate action plans, making it ideal for PE firms looking to actively drive decarbonization and value creation across their PortCos. It’s known for a fast setup and an intuitive experience.

    Key Features:

    • Cloud-based carbon accounting (Scopes 1, 2, and 3).
    • Guided decarbonization tools and climate action planning.
    • Supports multiple frameworks (GHG Protocol, CDP).
    • Customizable offerings for PE firms and their underlying PortCos.

    Pros:

    • Ease of Use: User-friendly interface, making it easy for non-ESG experts at PortCos to contribute data.
    • Action-Oriented: Moves beyond reporting to actively assist in setting and achieving science-based targets.
    • Strong option for net-zero strategy implementation.

    Cons:

    • Reporting functionality is sometimes viewed as less robust than enterprise-level GRC tools.
    • Pricing requires a tailored quote.

    Verdict: Sustain.Life is the pragmatic choice for PE firms whose primary mandate is to measure, manage, and actively reduce the carbon footprint of their portfolio companies.

    4. AuditBoard: For the Connected Risk Office

    AuditBoard is a unified Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platform. While it doesn’t specialize in ESG data collection like Novata, it excels as an alternative to Workiva because it connects ESG reporting directly with internal audit, SOX compliance, and risk management workflows—a key need for PE firms preparing PortCos for eventual IPO or M&A diligence.

    Key Features:

    • Unified GRC platform (Internal Audit, SOX, Risk, ESG).
    • Centralized data collection that flows directly into risk and control matrices.
    • Automated task management and framework mapping.
    • User-friendly interface for auditors and risk teams.

    Pros:

    • Workflow Integration: Connects ESG risk directly into existing GRC programs.
    • Unified Platform: Consolidates multiple risk functions into a single system, similar to Workiva’s appeal.
    • Ideal for firms prioritizing internal controls and audit readiness over pure emissions tracking.

    Cons:

    • High, opaque pricing structure (median contract value ~$42k/year).
    • ESG features are an add-on to the core GRC suite, potentially less specialized for pure sustainability needs.

    Verdict: Select AuditBoard if your firm needs to integrate ESG data not just for external reporting, but for internal audit, risk assessment, and SOX preparation across its investment portfolio.

    IBM Envizi is the enterprise solution for firms with extremely high data volumes, multiple facilities, and distributed energy/utilities data. While the “IBM” brand suggests large enterprise, its robust data engine is highly effective for PE firms managing a large, complex portfolio of asset-intensive companies.

    Key Features:

    • AI-powered data capture engine for distributed sources (automates data transformation).
    • GHG Protocol-compliant emissions calculation engine (Scopes 1, 2, and 3).
    • Embedded reporting framework questions (CDP, SASB, TCFD).
    • Powerful analytics and planning tools.

    Pros:

    • Data Accuracy & Scale: Systemizes data capture across disparate sources for a consistent, auditable dataset.
    • Integration: Backed by IBM, offering deep integration capabilities with existing enterprise systems.
    • Strong compliance focus, reducing manual work and ensuring accuracy.

    Cons:

    • Can be overly complex and expensive for smaller mid-market firms.
    • Implementation is significant, comparable to Workiva.

    Verdict: IBM Envizi is the best alternative if your PE firm’s portfolio contains many large, asset-heavy companies (e.g., manufacturing, real estate) where data collection and calculation complexity is the primary challenge.

    Conclusion: Making the Right Choice

    While Workiva offers a broad, enterprise-level solution, the mid-market private equity firm benefits most from specialization.

    For the vast majority of mid-market PE firms, Novata provides the most direct, purpose-built alternative to Workiva, offering simple data collection, benchmarking, and an essential private market focus.

    However, if your firm is focused on value creation through decarbonization, Sustain.Life offers a more action-oriented platform. If preparing your PortCos for complex future assurance is the goal, Pulsora’s auditability features stand above the rest.

    Assess your firm’s main pain point—is it compliance, investor reporting, or value creation—to select the best platform for your needs.
