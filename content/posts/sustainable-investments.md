# Comparison of Sustainable Investment Strategies

Sustainable Investments (SI) encompass a broad range of approaches focused on integrating Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) factors into investment decisions. This report compares the primary methodologies.

## Key Investment Methodologies

| Strategy | Primary Focus | Implementation Detail |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ESG Integration** | Systematic inclusion of ESG data in financial analysis. | Broadest approach; used across asset classes. |
| **Exclusionary Screening** | Avoiding specific sectors (e.g., tobacco, weapons, fossil fuels). | A foundational, values-based screening method. |
| **Thematic Investing** | Targeting investments in specific sustainability themes (e.g., clean energy, water). | Focuses on long-term trends linked to sustainability. |
| **Impact Investing** | Seeking measurable, positive social/environmental impact alongside financial return. | Requires rigorous tracking of non-financial metrics. |

## Performance Considerations

While historically viewed as a trade-off, modern SI often correlates positively with long-term risk-adjusted returns, primarily by mitigating regulatory and reputational risks associated with poor ESG performance. Impact Investing, while targeting measurable outcomes, requires careful management to ensure the financial return component is not compromised.
